All,

On site now. Spill has been cleaned up safely. Summary:

PRODUCT: Sika Poxitar F Two-Part Epoxy Primer (Bright Coat Services)
SDS confirmed: Section 6 — use inert absorbent material (sand, dry earth, or
vermiculite). Oil-only pads are NOT appropriate. We used the dry sand from the
Zone 1 spill kit, which is rated for this type of product.

PPE for cleanup: Nitrile gloves (minimum 0.3mm thickness), safety goggles,
and P2 respirator due to solvent vapour. Brendan and Dave Chen both wore
appropriate PPE for the cleanup — they did well.

QUANTITY: Approximately 14 litres recovered. Contained fully on concrete —
no drainage entry, no soil contact. Not an environmental incident requiring
external reporting.

WHAT WENT WRONG:
1. The 20L drum was not re-sealed after morning use. The Bright Coat crew had
   been working from the drum all morning and the lid was finger-tight only.
   Their SWMS for painting (BC-SWMS-04) specifies drums must be fully sealed
   when not actively in use. This was not followed.
2. The Zone 3 spill kit does not include sand or vermiculite — only oil-absorbent
   pads. This is a gap. Epoxy is used across Zones 2, 3, and 4 on this project.

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS:
1. All Bright Coat workers re-briefed on drum sealing requirements (BC-SWMS-04)
   before returning to painting tasks — Dave Chen / today
2. Spill kits in Zones 2, 3, and 4 to be supplemented with 10kg dry sand in
   a sealed bucket, labelled for epoxy/solvent spills — Tom Nguyen / 26 Feb 2024
3. BuildCore to review Bright Coat's SWMS BC-SWMS-04 with them to confirm
   understanding and re-sign — Jason / 27 Feb 2024
4. Add "drum sealing" as an observation item in the daily site inspection
   checklist (SSIC-001) — Jason / 27 Feb 2024

WASTE DISPOSAL: Contaminated absorbent sand has been bagged, labelled as
chemical waste, and placed in the site chemical waste storage area pending
licensed waste contractor collection (Enviro Collect, booked for Friday 1 March).

Incident report (IR-2024-009) submitted to safety file.

Jason
