Investigation complete. Near-Miss Investigation Report (Form NM-2024-007)
has been submitted to the safety file. Summary of findings:

ROOT CAUSES:
1. Inadequate exclusion zone marking — Zone B western boundary markings had
   faded to the point of being indistinguishable. Last formal inspection of
   line markings was November 2023 (3 months prior). Inspection frequency
   should be monthly, not quarterly.
2. Induction gap — Subcontractor induction refers to "marked exclusion zones"
   but does not provide a site map identifying specific zones by name or number.
   Pete Carmichael passed induction in good faith but had insufficient information
   to identify Zone B as a restricted area.

CONTRIBUTING FACTORS:
- FLT-03 travel alarm was operational (confirmed via pre-start log and physical
  inspection post-incident). However, the noise level in Zone B during the incident
  (Apex Plumbing running a concrete saw nearby) may have masked the alarm.
- No physical barriers (bollards or barriers) on the Zone B western boundary —
  line markings alone are not adequate control for an active forklift zone.

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS (owner / due date):
1. Repaint all Zone B exclusion zone markings — Tom Nguyen / 13 Feb 2024
2. Install 6 x removable bollards with chain on Zone B western boundary — Tom / 20 Feb 2024

   checklist — Jason / 15 Feb 2024

   areas — Tom / 13 Feb 2024
