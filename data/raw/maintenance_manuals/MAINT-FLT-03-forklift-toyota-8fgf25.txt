SECTION 1 — DAILY PRE-START CHECK PROCEDURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The pre-start check must be completed before the first use of each
operating shift. Use Form PSCH-FLT-03 and retain completed forms for
12 months.

Estimated time: 10–15 minutes.

Before beginning the check, ensure:
  - The forklift is parked on level ground with forks lowered
  - The engine is OFF
  - The parking brake is engaged

STEP 1 — LPG Cylinder Inspection
  1.1 Check the LPG cylinder gauge. If below 20% capacity, swap the
      cylinder before starting the shift. Spare cylinders are stored
      in the designated LPG cage at the site compound.
  1.2 Inspect the cylinder for dents, corrosion, or valve damage.
      Do not use a damaged cylinder.
  1.3 Check the LPG hose connection at the cylinder and at the engine.
      Apply soapy water to connections — any bubbling indicates a gas
      leak. Do not start the engine if a leak is detected. Tag out and
      notify the Site Manager immediately.
  1.4 Ensure the cylinder is secured in its mount and the strap or chain
      retainer is fastened.

STEP 2 — Fluid Levels (Engine Off)
  2.1 Engine oil — remove dipstick, wipe clean, re-insert, remove and read.
      Level must be between MIN and MAX marks.
      Top up with SAE 10W-30 if below MIN.
  2.2 Coolant — check overflow reservoir. Level must be between LOW and FULL.
      Do NOT open the radiator cap when the engine is hot.
  2.3 Hydraulic oil — check sight glass on the hydraulic reservoir (right side
      of unit). Level must be visible in the glass. Top up with Dexron III ATF.
  2.4 Brake fluid — check reservoir under the floorboard access panel.
      Level must be between MIN and MAX.

STEP 3 — Tyres
  3.1 Inspect all four tyres for:
      - Cuts, gouges, or embedded objects
      - Excessive wear (tread depth below 3mm — replace)
      - Chunking or cracking on solid tyres
  3.2 Drive tyres (rear): check tyre pressure — correct pressure is 100 psi
      (690 kPa). Use the tyre gauge in the site tool store.
  3.3 Steer tyres (front): FLT-03 has solid steer tyres — check for wear
      and physical damage only.

STEP 4 — Mast, Forks, and Carriage
  4.1 Inspect both forks for:
      - Cracks or fractures, especially at the heel and blade
      - Bend — place a straight edge along the blade. Any visible bow means
        remove from service immediately
      - Fork spread — measure from outside face of left fork to outside face
        of right fork. Must match the rated spread (±0.5%)
      - Wear at the heel — thickness must not be less than 90% of original
  4.2 Check fork locking pins are present and secured.
  4.3 Inspect mast chains — no broken links, kinking, corrosion, or
      elongation. Chains must be lubricated (light chain lube).
  4.4 Check mast rollers and carriage rollers for wear or missing rollers.

STEP 5 — Operator Controls (Engine Running)
  Start the engine and allow to idle for 2 minutes before performing checks.

  5.1 Horn — press horn button. Must be audible at 10m.
  5.2 Travel alarm — engage reverse. Audible reverse alarm must sound.
  5.3 Seat belt — fasten and unfasten. Buckle must engage cleanly.
  5.4 Lights — if working in reduced light: check headlights, tail lights,
      and beacon.
  5.5 Lift function — raise and lower forks through full range.
      Movement must be smooth with no jerking or hesitation.
  5.6 Tilt function — tilt mast forward and backward. Must move freely.
  5.7 Service brake — drive slowly forward and apply brakes. Unit must
      stop straight without pulling to one side.
  5.8 Parking brake — with engine running in neutral, apply parking brake
      and attempt to move forward at low speed. Unit must not move.

STEP 6 — General Visual
  6.1 Check for fluid leaks under the unit — oil, coolant, or hydraulic
      fluid on the ground.
  6.2 Check overhead guard is secure — no cracks, missing bolts, or deformation.
  6.3 Check seat and restraint belt are not damaged.
  6.4 Ensure data plate is legible (rated capacity at full mast height must
      be visible at all times).

If any check item FAILS: Do not operate. Attach OUT OF SERVICE tag to
steering wheel. Complete the pre-start form, mark the failed item, and
notify the Site Manager.

SECTION 2 — WEEKLY MAINTENANCE (OPERATOR)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The following items are checked weekly by the allocated operator in
addition to the daily pre-start. Record on Form WPSCH-FLT-03.

STEP 1 — Radiator
  Inspect radiator fins for blockage by dust or debris.
  If blocked, blow out with compressed air (engine cold, from inside out).

STEP 2 — Battery (Engine Starting Battery)
  Check terminal connections — must be tight and free of corrosion.
  Clean corrosion with a baking soda and water solution if present.

STEP 3 — Air Filter
  Remove air filter element cover. Inspect element for excessive dust loading.
  Tap gently to dislodge loose dust. If element is torn or heavily loaded,
  replace with Toyota 8FGF25 OEM element (Part No. 17700-23330-71).

STEP 4 — Chain Lubrication
  Apply a light coat of chain lubricant to both mast lift chains.
  Do not use heavy grease — attracts debris and accelerates wear.

SECTION 5 — EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

5.1 Forklift Tip-Over
  DO NOT JUMP. If a tip-over occurs:
  Step 1: Grip the steering wheel firmly with both hands.
  Step 2: Brace feet on the floor.
  Step 3: Lean away from the direction of fall and hold on.
  The overhead guard is designed to protect the operator.
  Jumping increases injury risk significantly.

5.2 LPG Fire
  Step 1: Shut off the LPG cylinder valve immediately if safe to do so.
  Step 2: Evacuate the area — 15m minimum exclusion zone.
  Step 3: Call 000. Do not attempt to fight an LPG fire.
  Step 4: Notify the Site Manager.

5.3 Pedestrian Strike
  Step 1: Stop the forklift immediately.
  Step 2: Call 000 if any injury has occurred.
  Step 3: Do not move the forklift until directed by emergency services
          or the Site Manager.
  Step 4: Preserve the scene. The forklift is evidence.
