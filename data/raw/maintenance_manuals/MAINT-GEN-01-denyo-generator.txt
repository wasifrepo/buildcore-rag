WARNING — CARBON MONOXIDE HAZARD
GEN-01 produces carbon monoxide (CO), a colourless, odourless, and
potentially fatal gas. Never operate GEN-01 indoors, in a partially
enclosed space, or within 6 metres of any door, window, or air intake.

WARNING — ELECTRICAL HAZARD
GEN-01 produces 415V three-phase and 240V single-phase power. All
electrical connections to GEN-01 must be made by a licensed electrician.
Never connect GEN-01 to a fixed installation without an approved isolation
device to prevent backfeed to the grid.

WARNING — FIRE HAZARD
Diesel fuel is flammable. Do not refuel while GEN-01 is running or hot.
No smoking within 6 metres of GEN-01 during refuelling.

SECTION 2 — DAILY PRE-START CHECK PROCEDURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Complete Form PSCH-GEN-01 before each day of operation.
Estimated time: 10 minutes. Perform with generator OFF.

STEP 1 — Site Positioning Check
  1.1 Confirm GEN-01 is positioned on a firm, level surface.
  1.2 Confirm minimum 6m clearance from all doors, windows, and air intakes.
  1.3 Confirm exhaust outlet is directed away from work areas and site offices.
  1.4 Confirm GEN-01 is within the fenced/barricaded generator compound area.
      Unauthorised access to the compound must be prevented at all times.

STEP 2 — Fuel Level
  2.1 Check fuel gauge on the instrument panel.
  2.2 If fuel is below 25% (approximately 26 litres), refuel before starting.
  2.3 Use diesel fuel only — filling with petrol will cause serious engine damage.
  2.4 After refuelling, ensure the fuel cap is fully closed and locked.
  2.5 Record fuel used and added in the GEN-01 fuel log (Form FL-GEN-01).

STEP 3 — Engine Oil
  3.1 Locate dipstick (yellow handle, right side of engine compartment).
  3.2 Wipe dipstick clean, re-insert fully, remove and read.
  3.3 Oil level must be between the MIN and MAX marks.
  3.4 If below MIN: top up with SAE 15W-40 diesel engine oil.
      Do not overfill — overfilling causes oil seal damage.

STEP 4 — Coolant Level
  4.1 Check the coolant overflow reservoir (translucent tank, left side).
  4.2 Level must be between the LOW and FULL marks.
  4.3 Top up with pre-mixed 50/50 coolant (green, ethylene glycol based).
  4.4 Never open the radiator cap when the engine is warm.

STEP 5 — Visual Inspection
  5.1 Check for any fluid leaks (oil, coolant, diesel) on the ground beneath
      GEN-01. Investigate and rectify any leak before starting.
  5.2 Check all panel doors are closed and latched.
  5.3 Check exhaust system — no blockages, no loose connections.
  5.4 Check output cables — no damage, kinks, or exposed conductors.
  5.5 Check all cable glands on the output panel are secure.

STEP 6 — Control Panel Check (before start)
  6.1 Set the AUTO/MANUAL switch to MANUAL.
  6.2 Confirm the output circuit breaker is in the OFF position.
  6.3 Confirm the EMERGENCY STOP button is released (turn clockwise to release).

STEP 7 — Starting Procedure
  7.1 Set choke to COLD START position if ambient temperature is below 10°C.
  7.2 Turn the key to START. Hold until engine fires (maximum 10 seconds).
      If engine does not start within 10 seconds, release key and wait 30 seconds
      before attempting again. Maximum 3 attempts.
  7.3 Allow engine to idle for 2 minutes before applying load.
  7.4 Return choke to RUN position after 30 seconds if used.
  7.5 Check oil pressure warning light — must extinguish within 5 seconds of start.
      If light remains on, shut down immediately. Do not operate.
  7.6 Check coolant temperature gauge — must reach operating range (70-90°C)
      within 5 minutes of startup.
  7.7 Close the output circuit breaker to apply load to connected equipment.

SECTION 3 — SHUTDOWN PROCEDURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

STEP 1: Open the output circuit breaker to remove load.
STEP 2: Allow GEN-01 to run at idle (no load) for 3 minutes to cool down.
STEP 3: Turn key to OFF position.
STEP 4: Confirm the engine has stopped completely before leaving the unit.
STEP 5: Record run hours from the hour meter in the fuel/run log (Form FL-GEN-01).
STEP 6: Lock the fuel cap and close all panel doors.

EMERGENCY STOP: Press the red EMERGENCY STOP button on the control panel.
Use only in an emergency. After an emergency stop, investigate the cause
before restarting. Record the incident in the maintenance log.
