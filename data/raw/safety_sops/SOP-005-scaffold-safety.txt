1. PURPOSE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

To establish minimum requirements for the safe erection, use, inspection,
alteration, and dismantling of scaffolding systems at BuildCore sites in
compliance with OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart Q.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
2. SCAFFOLD CLASSIFICATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

BuildCore sites use the following scaffold types. Each type requires
the specific controls noted.

  Type A — Tube and Coupler Scaffolding
    Max load: 25 psf. Requires engineer sign-off for heights over 125 ft.
    Inspection frequency: Every shift.

  Type B — Frame / Fabricated Scaffolding (most common on BuildCore sites)
    Max load: 25 psf light duty; 50 psf medium duty; 75 psf heavy duty.
    Pre-tagged with load rating by supplier. Tag must remain visible.
    Inspection frequency: Every shift.

  Type C — Suspended Scaffolding (swing stage)
    Requires separate engineering design. Not covered by this SOP.
    Refer to SOP-005A (Suspended Scaffold).

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
3. COMPETENT PERSON REQUIREMENTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Only a designated Competent Person may:
  - Supervise scaffold erection and dismantling
  - Conduct pre-shift inspections and authorise scaffold for use
  - Determine when a scaffold is unsafe and must be taken out of service

The Competent Person must hold a current BuildCore Scaffold Competency
Certificate (renewed every 2 years). List of certified personnel is
maintained by the Safety Manager (Form SC-001).

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
4. ERECTION PROCEDURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 Pre-Erection Planning
  Step 1: Review site plan and confirm scaffold footprint does not
          obstruct emergency exits, fire equipment, or traffic lanes.
  Step 2: Confirm ground bearing capacity. Soft or uneven ground
          requires mudsills (minimum 2 x 10 lumber, 18 inches long).
  Step 3: Check overhead hazards — electrical lines must be de-energised
          or have minimum clearances per OSHA 1926.416.
  Step 4: Barricade the erection zone with barrier tape. No unauthorised
          personnel within 6 ft of erection activity.

4.2 Base and Foundation
  Step 1: Place mudsills flat on compacted ground.
  Step 2: Position base plates centred on mudsills.
  Step 3: Install screw jacks — extension must not exceed 12 inches.
          If ground is not level, adjust jacks before proceeding.

4.3 Frame Installation
  Step 1: Erect first level frames and connect with cross braces.
          Braces must be locked on both ends.
  Step 2: Check plumb and level before adding the second level.
          Maximum out-of-plumb: 1 inch per 10 feet of height.
  Step 3: Install coupling pins between stacked frames.
          Coupling pins must be secured with a pin or clip.
  Step 4: Repeat for each level, checking plumb at every second level.

4.4 Guardrails and Toeboards
  Step 1: Install top guardrail at 38-45 inches above platform.
  Step 2: Install midrail at the midpoint of top rail and platform.
  Step 3: Install toeboards (minimum 3.5 inches) on all open sides.
  Step 4: All open sides and ends of the platform must be guarded.

4.5 Platform Planking
  Step 1: Planks must be scaffold-grade or equivalent structural lumber.
  Step 2: Planks must extend 6-18 inches beyond supports.
  Step 3: Planks must overlap at least 12 inches over supports.
  Step 4: Planks must be secured to prevent displacement.

4.6 Access
  Ladders or stairway frames must be provided.
  Climbing on cross-braces is prohibited.

5. PRE-SHIFT INSPECTION CHECKLIST
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The Competent Person must complete Form SC-002 (Scaffold Pre-Shift
Inspection) before each shift. The scaffold must NOT be used until the
inspection is complete and the scaffold is signed off as SAFE.

Inspection items — mark PASS / FAIL / N/A:

  FOUNDATION
  [ ] Mudsills and base plates in place, no settlement or movement
  [ ] Screw jack extensions within 12-inch limit
  [ ] Ground beneath scaffold has not changed (soft spots, erosion)

  FRAMES AND BRACING
  [ ] All frames upright, no visible bends or deformation
  [ ] All cross braces locked at both ends
  [ ] Coupling pins secured on all stacked frames
  [ ] Scaffold plumb within 1 inch per 10 feet

  PLATFORM
  [ ] Planks free of cracks, knots, rot, or damage
  [ ] Planks overlap minimum 12 inches at supports
  [ ] No gaps greater than 1 inch between planks
  [ ] Platform free of debris, tools, or excess materials

  GUARDRAILS AND TOEBOARDS
  [ ] Top rail at 38-45 inches, no deflection under 200 lb load
  [ ] Midrail present and secure
  [ ] Toeboards minimum 3.5 inches on all open sides

  ACCESS
  [ ] Ladder or stair access present and secure
  [ ] No climbing on braces observed

  TAGS AND SIGNAGE
  [ ] Load rating tag visible and legible
  [ ] Scaffold tag (green = safe, red = do not use) current

  If any item FAILS: scaffold must be tagged RED (Do Not Use) and
  Competent Person must notify the Site Safety Manager immediately.

6. USE RESTRICTIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  - Do not exceed rated load capacity (shown on load tag)
  - Do not use scaffold during electrical storms or winds over 65 km/h
  - Do not move a scaffold while workers are on it (mobile scaffolds only
    permitted to be moved if properly designed for movement with workers)
  - Do not use scaffold as a support point for hoisting equipment unless
    specifically designed and approved in writing by a professional engineer
  - Do not alter, remove, or modify any scaffold component without
    authorisation from the Competent Person

7. DISMANTLING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Dismantling is the reverse of erection but carries additional risk.

  - Barricade the dismantling zone — no unauthorised access
  - Remove tools, materials, and debris from platform before dismantling
  - Dismantle from the top down — never remove lower components first
  - Lower materials to the ground using ropes or mechanical means —
    do not throw or drop components
  - Damaged or defective components must be tagged and segregated
