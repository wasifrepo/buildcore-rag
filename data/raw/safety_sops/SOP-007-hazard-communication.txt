1. PURPOSE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

This SOP establishes BuildCore's Hazard Communication Program in compliance
with OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1200 (HazCom 2012 / GHS). It ensures all employees
are informed about the hazardous chemicals they may be exposed to, and that
appropriate controls are in place to protect their health and safety.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
2. SCOPE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Applies to all BuildCore employees and subcontractors who may be exposed to
hazardous chemicals in the course of their work, including but not limited to:
  - Paints, coatings, and solvents
  - Adhesives, sealants, and epoxies
  - Concrete and masonry products
  - Cleaning and degreasing agents
  - Fuels, lubricants, and hydraulic fluids
  - Welding fumes and gases

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
3. SAFETY DATA SHEETS (SDS)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 Availability
  An SDS must be available for every hazardous chemical present on site.
  SDS documents are maintained in two locations:
    a) Physical binder: Site office / Safety Manager's desk
    b) Digital: BuildCore SharePoint > Safety > SDS Library

  SDS must be accessible to workers at all times during their shift.
  If an SDS is missing for a chemical on site, work involving that
  chemical must stop until the SDS is obtained.

3.2 Obtaining SDS
  - New chemicals: Supplier must provide SDS before first shipment
  - Subcontractor chemicals: Subcontractor must submit SDS to Safety
    Manager before bringing any hazardous chemical on site
  - SDS older than 3 years must be verified with the manufacturer

3.3 16-Section SDS Format (GHS)
  Workers must be able to locate the following critical sections:

  Section 2  — Hazard Identification (signal words, pictograms)
  Section 4  — First Aid Measures
  Section 6  — Accidental Release Measures (spill response)
  Section 7  — Handling and Storage
  Section 8  — Exposure Controls / PPE requirements
  Section 14 — Transport Information

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
4. CHEMICAL LABELLING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 Manufacturer Labels
  All chemicals must remain in their original labelled containers.
  Labels must include:
    - Product identifier
    - Signal word (DANGER or WARNING)
    - Hazard statements
    - Precautionary statements
    - GHS pictograms
    - Supplier information

4.2 Secondary Container Labels
  Chemicals transferred to secondary containers must be labelled
  with at minimum:
    - Product name
    - Primary hazard(s) (e.g., "FLAMMABLE", "CORROSIVE")
    - Relevant GHS pictograms

  NOTE: Portable containers used by a single worker during a single
  shift do not require a secondary label, provided the worker retains
  the SDS and original container nearby.

4.3 Prohibited Practices
  - Removing or defacing any chemical label
  - Using unlabelled containers for any hazardous substance
  - Storing chemicals in food or beverage containers

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
5. CHEMICAL INVENTORY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The Site Safety Manager maintains a current Chemical Inventory (Form CH-001)
updated at minimum monthly. The inventory records:
  - Chemical name and product identifier
  - Supplier / manufacturer
  - Location on site
  - Approximate quantity
  - SDS version date

Subcontractors must submit their own chemical inventory to the Safety
Manager within 48 hours of mobilisation on site.

6. EXPOSURE CONTROLS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

6.1 Hierarchy of Controls
  Controls must be applied in the following order of preference:
    1. Substitution — replace hazardous chemical with a less hazardous one
    2. Engineering controls — ventilation, enclosure, local exhaust
    3. Administrative controls — work scheduling, rotation, signage
    4. PPE — gloves, respirators, eye protection (last resort)

6.2 Permissible Exposure Limits (PELs)
  BuildCore adopts OSHA PELs as the minimum standard. Where ACGIH
  Threshold Limit Values (TLVs) are more protective, TLVs apply.
  Site Safety Manager maintains a list of chemicals with PELs relevant
  to current site activities.

6.3 Respiratory Protection
  Where inhalation exposure is a risk, respiratory protection is
  selected per SOP-014 (Respiratory Protection Program).

7. SPILL RESPONSE PROCEDURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

7.1 Immediate Actions (All Workers)
  Step 1: Alert others in the area and evacuate the immediate spill zone.
  Step 2: Identify the chemical — check label or SDS (Section 6).
  Step 3: Do NOT attempt to clean up if the chemical is unknown,
          highly toxic, flammable, or if you are not trained.
  Step 4: Call the Site Safety Manager immediately.

7.2 Minor Spills (Safety Manager or Trained Personnel Only)
  Step 1: Don appropriate PPE per the SDS (Section 8).
  Step 2: Contain the spill using absorbent materials from the spill kit.
          Do not wash spills into drains.
  Step 3: Collect all contaminated absorbent material in a labelled
          waste container for hazardous waste disposal.
  Step 4: Ventilate the area until vapour levels are safe.
  Step 5: Complete Incident Report Form IR-001 within 24 hours.

7.3 Major Spills / Unknown Chemicals
  Evacuate, secure the area, and call emergency services (911).
  Do not re-enter until cleared by emergency responders.

8. TRAINING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

8.1 Initial HazCom Training (Course HC-100)
  Required before any worker may be exposed to hazardous chemicals.
  Covers:
    - How to read a GHS label
    - How to locate and use an SDS
    - Physical and health hazards of site chemicals
    - Protective measures and PPE
    - Spill response procedures

8.2 Chemical-Specific Training
  When new hazardous chemicals are introduced, all potentially
  exposed workers must receive specific training before first use.

8.3 Retraining
  Required when a new hazard is introduced, or when a worker
  demonstrates inadequate knowledge of HazCom requirements.
