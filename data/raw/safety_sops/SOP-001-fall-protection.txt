1. PURPOSE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the requirements for fall
protection at all BuildCore Operations construction and facilities
management sites. It defines the minimum standards for identifying fall
hazards, selecting appropriate fall protection systems, and ensuring all
personnel are trained and equipped before working at heights.

This SOP applies to all employees, subcontractors, and visitors working
at any BuildCore site where a fall hazard of 1.8 metres (6 feet) or
greater exists.

2. SCOPE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

This procedure covers all work activities involving potential fall hazards,
including but not limited to:

  - Rooftop work and maintenance
  - Scaffold erection and dismantling
  - Ladder use (portable and fixed)
  - Work near floor openings, wall openings, and excavations
  - Steel erection
  - Leading edge work
  - Elevated platforms and mezzanines

4. DEFINITIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Anchorage Point
  A secure point of attachment for lifelines, lanyards, or deceleration
  devices. Must support at least 5,000 lbs (22.2 kN) per attached worker.

Free Fall Distance
  The vertical displacement of the fall arrest attachment point from its
  static position to the point where the system begins to apply fall
  arrest forces. Must not exceed 1.8 m (6 ft).

Personal Fall Arrest System (PFAS)
  A system used to arrest an employee in a fall from a working level.
  Consists of an anchorage, connectors, a body harness, and may include
  a lanyard, deceleration device, lifeline, or suitable combinations.

Leading Edge
  The unprotected side and edge of a floor, roof, or formwork that changes
  location as additional floor, roof, decking, or formwork sections are
  placed, formed, or constructed.

Competent Person
  An individual capable of identifying existing and predictable fall hazards
  and who has authority to take prompt corrective action to eliminate them.

5. RESPONSIBILITIES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

5.1 Site Safety Manager
  - Develop and maintain this SOP
  - Conduct site-specific fall hazard assessments prior to work commencement
  - Ensure all fall protection equipment is inspected and certified
  - Investigate all fall-related incidents and near-misses
  - Maintain training records for all personnel

5.2 Supervisors and Foremen
  - Conduct daily pre-task planning to identify fall hazards
  - Ensure workers under their supervision are trained and equipped
  - Stop work immediately if fall protection is inadequate
  - Report any defective equipment to the Safety Manager

5.3 Workers
  - Inspect personal fall protection equipment before each use
  - Use fall protection as required by this SOP
  - Report fall hazards, near-misses, and equipment defects to their supervisor
  - Never disable, remove, or bypass fall protection systems

5.4 Subcontractors
  - Comply with all BuildCore fall protection requirements
  - Provide documentation of worker training upon request
  - Ensure their own equipment meets ANSI Z359.1 standards

6. FALL PROTECTION REQUIREMENTS BY WORK TYPE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

6.1 General Requirement
  Fall protection is mandatory whenever a worker is exposed to a fall
  of 1.8 m (6 ft) or greater to a lower level. The hierarchy of fall
  protection controls must be applied in this order:

    1. Elimination — redesign the work to remove the fall hazard
    2. Passive systems — guardrail systems, safety net systems
    3. Active systems — personal fall arrest systems (PFAS)
    4. Administrative controls — warning line systems, safety monitors
       (only permitted where other controls are not feasible)

6.2 Rooftop Work
  6.2.1 Guardrail System (preferred)
    - Top rail height: 42 inches (± 3 inches) above walking surface
    - Mid rail: at the midpoint between top rail and walking surface
    - Capable of withstanding 200 lbs of outward or downward force
    - All roof perimeters must be guarded before workers access the roof

  6.2.2 Personal Fall Arrest System (alternative)
    - Full-body harness mandatory — no body belts permitted
    - Lanyard must be self-retracting or shock-absorbing type
    - Anchorage point must be above the D-ring of the harness
    - Total fall distance including deceleration must not exceed 18.5 ft
    - Workers must be able to be rescued within 20 minutes of arrest

  6.2.3 Prohibited on rooftops
    - Work during wind speeds exceeding 50 km/h
    - Work on wet or icy surfaces without anti-slip measures
    - Use of body belts as fall arrest (positioning only)

6.3 Scaffolding (see also SOP-005)
  - Scaffold platforms must have guardrails on all open sides and ends
  - Toeboards minimum 3.5 inches high on all sides
  - Scaffold must be inspected by a Competent Person each shift
  - Workers on scaffolds over 10 ft must use PFAS if guardrails are absent

6.4 Ladders (see also SOP-012)
  - Three points of contact maintained at all times
  - No carrying of materials that impair grip or balance
  - Extension ladders must extend 3 ft above the landing point
  - Ladder feet must be secured or held by a second person

6.5 Floor and Wall Openings
  - All floor openings must be covered or guarded immediately upon creation
  - Covers must be marked "HOLE — DO NOT REMOVE" and capable of supporting
    twice the intended load
  - Wall openings 30 inches or more in height and 18 inches or more in
    width at 6 ft or more above grade require guardrails

6.6 Excavations
  - Workers entering excavations greater than 4 ft deep require access
    and egress within 25 ft of lateral travel
  - Trenches and excavations must be protected per SOP-019 (Excavation Safety)

7. EQUIPMENT INSPECTION PROCEDURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

7.1 Pre-Use Inspection (Worker Responsibility — Before Every Use)

  7.1.1 Full-Body Harness
    Step 1: Hold harness by the back D-ring and shake. Let the harness
            unfold to its proper position.
    Step 2: Check all webbing for cuts, burns, abrasion, fraying, or
            chemical damage. Run fingers along all webbing.
    Step 3: Check all hardware (buckles, D-rings) for deformation,
            cracks, corrosion, or sharp edges.
    Step 4: Check stitching on all load-bearing points — no broken or
            missing stitches permitted.
    Step 5: Check labels — ANSI Z359.1 compliance label must be legible.
    Step 6: If any defect is found, tag the harness OUT OF SERVICE and
            return to the Safety Manager immediately.

  7.1.2 Lanyards and Self-Retracting Lifelines (SRLs)
    Step 1: Inspect snaphooks — gate must open freely and lock positively.
    Step 2: Check energy absorber — if deployment indicator shows red
            or orange, the lanyard has been shock-loaded and must be
            removed from service immediately.
    Step 3: Check lifeline for cuts, kinks, abrasion, or chemical damage.
    Step 4: For SRLs — pull out 12 inches of cable and release. The SRL
            should retract smoothly. Tug sharply — the lock must engage.

7.2 Annual Inspection
  All fall protection equipment must be inspected annually by a
  Competent Person and documented on Form SF-009 (Equipment Inspection Log).
  Equipment that fails annual inspection must be destroyed — not stored.

7.3 Post-Arrest Inspection
  Any equipment involved in a fall arrest event must be immediately
  removed from service and inspected by the manufacturer or a certified
  inspector before return to use. Equipment with visible damage after
  arrest must be destroyed.

8. TRAINING REQUIREMENTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

8.1 Initial Training
  All workers exposed to fall hazards must complete BuildCore Fall
  Protection Training (Course FP-100) before working at heights.
  Training must cover:
    - Fall hazard recognition
    - Selection and use of fall protection systems
    - Correct donning, doffing, and inspection of harnesses
    - Rescue procedures following a fall arrest

8.2 Retraining
  Retraining is required when:
    - A worker is observed using fall protection incorrectly
    - A near-miss or incident involving fall protection occurs
    - New equipment or procedures are introduced
    - More than 2 years have elapsed since last training

8.3 Documentation
  Training records must be retained for the duration of employment
  plus 3 years. Records are maintained by the Safety Manager on
  Form HR-022 (Training Record).

9. RESCUE PLAN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

A rescue plan must be in place before any work requiring PFAS begins.
The plan must address:

  - How a suspended worker will be retrieved within 20 minutes
  - Suspension trauma risk — workers must not remain suspended
    passively — orthostatic intolerance (suspension trauma) can
    cause loss of consciousness within minutes
  - Rescue equipment available on site (rescue ladder, descent device)
  - Names and contacts of trained rescue personnel on site

Post-rescue: All workers involved in a fall arrest must be assessed
by a medical professional before returning to work.

10. RECORDKEEPING AND REPORTING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  - All fall incidents and near-misses must be reported to the Site
    Safety Manager within 1 hour of occurrence
  - Incidents resulting in hospitalisation must be reported to OSHA
    within 24 hours per 29 CFR 1904.39
  - Completed inspection forms (SF-009) retained for 5 years
  - Training records (HR-022) retained for employment duration + 3 years
